When Distance Becomes Deadly


Author: Rod Waddington, 2014.
image Link
A child developed severe malaria in a rural community. What started as a simple fever quickly became life-threatening. The nearest clinic was far, and transportation was unavailable. By the time help arrived, it was too late. This is the reality for many, where access to care determines survival.

When Treatment is out of reach


Source: Jeannette, 2022
image link
An elderly man living with diabetes began to feel weak and dizzy. He had run out of his medication weeks ago. The clinic that supplied his drugs had shut down temporarily due to a lack of resources. With no money to travel to a bigger hospital, he stayed home, hoping to recover. His condition worsened silently. By the time his family realized how serious it was, it was too late. A manageable condition became a death sentence.

A Night That Took Two Lives


Source: Holly Lebowitz Rossi, 2024.
image link
A pregnant woman went into labor in the middle of the night. Complications began almost immediately. Her family tried to find transport, but no driver was willing to risk the bad roads in the dark. The nearest hospital was miles away. They waited, hoping the pain would pass. By morning, both mother and child were gone. A life that could have been saved was lost to distance and delay.
